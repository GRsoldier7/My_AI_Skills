# Community Builder — Savant-Level Skill

## Philosophy

Community mastery = **transformation over information** (members stay for who they become, not what they learn), **culture is the product** (the real deliverable is the relationships and identity), **the flywheel is the business model** (engaged members create content, recruit members, and buy more), and **10 raving fans beat 1,000 passive members** (depth over breadth at every stage).

---

## 1. Platform Selection

> **Note:** Community platform pricing and features change frequently. The criteria and trade-offs below are durable; specific prices and feature availability should be validated against current platform documentation before choosing.

### Platform Selection Criteria
```
Select your platform by answering these questions first:

1. Is monetization built into the platform, or will you bolt it on?
   → Native monetization (e.g., Skool, Circle): simpler setup; less flexibility
   → External payments (e.g., Memberful + Discord): more control; more complexity

2. Is the primary format chat (real-time) or forum (async, discoverable)?
   → Real-time chat (Discord-style): high energy; higher noise; requires moderation
   → Forum/posts (Skool/Circle-style): lower noise; content is searchable and persistent

3. What's your audience's existing platform habit?
   → Developers/creators/gamers: Discord has massive existing user base
   → Professionals/B2B: Slack familiarity; or dedicated platform for separation from work
   → Course + community bundle: platforms with native course player (Skool, Circle, Kajabi)

4. What does your monetization model require?
   → Paid membership with tiers: needs native payment gating or Memberful integration
   → Community as product add-on (e.g., SaaS user community): Discord or Slack is fine
   → High-ticket mastermind: private Slack or Circle with video rooms
```

### Platform Comparison Matrix (Validate Current Pricing)
```
Course-integrated community platforms (e.g., Skool):
  Best for: Paid communities bundling curriculum + discussion
  Key strengths: Built-in course player, gamification, chronological feed (no algorithm)
  Key weaknesses: Less customizable; platform lock-in risk
  Pricing model: Flat monthly fee for host; you set member pricing
  Sweet spot: $49-197/mo membership with course curriculum + community

Feature-rich community platforms (e.g., Circle):
  Best for: Mid-to-large paid communities wanting white-label look
  Key strengths: Custom domain, spaces, livestreaming, events, rich member profiles
  Key weaknesses: More setup required; can feel fragmented with too many spaces
  Pricing model: Tiered by features; you control member pricing
  Sweet spot: $49-297/mo for professional communities with events

Chat-first platforms (e.g., Discord):
  Best for: Free or low-cost communities; developer/creator/gaming audiences
  Key strengths: Free, real-time chat, voice channels, automation/bot ecosystem
  Key weaknesses: Noisy; notifications overwhelm; hard to monetize natively
  Pricing model: Free for host; gate paid roles via external payment integration
  Sweet spot: Free community with paid tier gateway, or pipeline to paid platform

Professional async platforms (e.g., Slack):
  Best for: Professional B2B communities; orgs with existing Slack adoption
  Key strengths: Familiar UX for professionals; strong integrations
  Key weaknesses: Expensive at scale; channels get cluttered; no native monetization
  Sweet spot: Internal team or customer success communities; not ideal for public paid
```

## 2. Community Flywheel Design

### The Engagement Flywheel
```
1. Members JOIN for the promise (transformation/access/content)
2. Members ENGAGE because of welcome + quick win
3. Members CONTRIBUTE because they feel safe and valued
4. Members RECRUIT because they're proud to be members
5. New members JOIN → flywheel spins

Flywheel accelerators:
  → Clear identity: Members say "I'm a [community name] member" with pride
  → Quick win: First meaningful interaction within 24h of joining
  → Public wins: Celebrate member success loudly
  → Referral reward: Structural incentive to bring in peers

Flywheel killers:
  ← Low onboarding: No greeting, no guidance, members ghost
  ← Spam/low-quality posts: Members stop opening notifications
  ← No moderation: Culture degrades without consistent standards
  ← Owner disappears: Community feels like an abandoned building
```

### Community Culture Architecture
```
Rules: 5 simple rules, not 20 complex ones
  Example rules:
  1. Be generous — share what you know
  2. Be specific — vague questions get vague answers; show your work
  3. No promo without contribution — earn the right to share your work
  4. Be kind, be real — no performance, no guru posturing
  5. Give before you take — comment on 10 posts before you ask for help

Rituals: Recurring events that create belonging
  Examples:
  Weekly: "Win Wednesday" — share one win, no matter how small
  Weekly: "Struggle Sunday" — ask the community for help with your current problem
  Monthly: Guest expert Q&A
  Quarterly: Community challenge (build something, get feedback)

Roles and status:
  Newcomer → Member → Contributor → Expert → Moderator
  Each level unlocks new channels, visibility, or perks
  Point systems (Skool does this natively) reward consistent contribution

Identity markers:
  Custom emojis, member badges, titles, "class of [year]" cohorts
  Goal: Members feel like they're part of something, not just subscribed to something
```

## 3. Monetization Models

### Community Revenue Architecture
```
Model 1: Paid Membership (simplest, most scalable)
  Structure: Monthly or annual subscription
  Tiers: One tier (simplest) or two (standard + premium)
  Price: $49-197/mo for B2B/professional; $15-49/mo for B2C
  Revenue: 500 members × $97/mo = $48,500 MRR
  Works when: Community itself is the product (transformation, access, network)

Model 2: Community + Course Bundle
  Structure: Paid membership includes evergreen course library
  Price: $97-297/mo or $497-1,997/year
  Works when: Curriculum is the reason people join; community is the support
  Example: Cohort-based learning programs, skilling communities

Model 3: Free Community + Paid Upsell
  Structure: Free community drives product/service sales
  Free: General community around your topic
  Paid: Your product (SaaS, consulting, course) for members who want more
  Works when: Community is a top-of-funnel acquisition channel

Model 4: Community as Service (for B2B)
  Structure: Community included with product subscription
  Drives: Retention, expansion, product feedback
  Works when: Community reduces churn by increasing switching cost + ROI

Model 5: Community-Driven Content/Events (media model)
  Revenue: Sponsorships, events, job boards
  Works when: Community has large, engaged audience in a valuable demographic

Pricing benchmarks:
  Entry ($15-49/mo): Broad audiences; content/inspiration focus
  Core ($49-197/mo): Professional/B2B; transformation + peer network
  Premium ($197-500/mo): High-income, high-stakes, peer accountability
  High-Ticket ($500-2K/mo): Elite mastermind; C-suite; serious practitioners
```

## 4. Onboarding System

### The First 7 Days
```
Day 0 (Join):
  □ Automated welcome DM from community leader
  □ Welcome post in introductions channel pinned at top
  □ "Start here" channel with 3 specific actions to take first
  □ Quick intro prompt: "Tell us: Who are you, what are you building,
    and what's your #1 challenge right now?"

Day 1-2:
  □ Community leader or moderator replies to their intro personally
  □ Introduce them to 1-2 existing members who share their context
  □ Surface one relevant resource or discussion thread for their challenge

Day 3-5:
  □ Check-in message: "How are you finding the community? Is there
    anything specific you're looking to get out of your membership?"
  □ Share one quick win they can implement immediately
  □ Invite to nearest community event or call

Day 7:
  □ "First week check-in" — did they get value?
  □ Collect their first feedback
  □ If not yet engaged: Re-activation with a specific question or intro

Member who doesn't engage in 14 days = at-risk.
Personalized outreach required before they quietly leave.
```

## 5. Scaling Challenges

### Community Quality Maintenance at Scale
```
0-100 members: Founder-moderated, personal attention to every member
100-500 members: 2-3 moderators, documented community standards
500-2K members: Volunteer moderator team, community guidelines expanded
2K+ members: Paid community manager, dedicated moderation policy

Quality decay signals:
  □ Response time to questions > 24 hours
  □ Promotional posts without value increasing
  □ Top contributors becoming less active
  □ New member comments going unreplied

Retention mechanics at scale:
  - Private "inner circle" for most engaged members
  - Rotating cohorts (new starting class each quarter — creates fresh energy)
  - Annual community summit or event (in-person if possible)
  - Member spotlight series (every member wants to be seen)
```

## Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Opening a Community Without a Seeded Culture**
Launching a public community with zero members, zero established norms, and zero initial content. New members arrive to an empty room, post once, hear crickets, and never return. The community dynamic never starts.
Fix: Pre-seed the community with 20-30 hand-picked "founding members" before public launch. Invite personally; give them a founding member badge and permanent status. Seed 2-3 weeks of discussions before opening to the public. The founding members create the energy that new members enter into.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Over-Channeling**
Creating 30+ channels/spaces "for everything" on Discord or Circle. Members don't know where to post, discussion gets fragmented, nothing feels active, and the noise overwhelms everyone.
Fix: Start with 5 channels maximum: Welcome/Intro, General Discussion, [Primary Topic], Resources/Tools, [One specialty channel]. Add channels only when an existing channel has consistent overflow. More channels = less engagement per channel.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Disappearing After Launch**
Launching the community, selling memberships, then showing up infrequently. Members see the host engaging less, feel like they're paying for an abandoned space, and stop paying. Community death is usually driven by founder disengagement before member disengagement.
Fix: Commit to a minimum weekly presence: one post, 10 comments, one event per month. Block it on the calendar. If you can't commit 30 minutes per day to the community you're charging for, either hire a community manager before you launch or don't launch.

## Quality Gates

- [ ] Platform selected with documented reason (matches audience, monetization model, and size stage)
- [ ] Flywheel designed: join → engage → contribute → recruit cycle mapped
- [ ] Onboarding system implemented: automated welcome + human touchpoint in first 48h
- [ ] Weekly ritual defined: recurring event that creates culture and gives members reason to return
- [ ] Monetization model matches community stage and audience (don't over-price for audience size)
- [ ] Content moderation standards documented: what's allowed, what's removed, what's celebrated

## Failure Modes and Fallbacks

**Failure: Community engagement drops 60 days after launch**
Detection: First 30 days great. Day 60: posts unanswered, new members not introducing themselves, old members going quiet. The "post-launch slump."
Fallback: Post-launch slump is universal. Fix: (1) Run a community challenge with prizes for engagement (reactivates dormant members), (2) Interview 5 engaged members and 5 lapsed members — understand the gap, (3) Launch a monthly live event (video call, Q&A, workshop) — scheduled events create forward pull, (4) Create a "member win" campaign: DM 20 members asking for one recent win to share publicly.

**Failure: Strong engagement but no paying members**
Detection: Free Discord or community has 500+ engaged members. No one is converting to paid product/course/premium tier.
Fallback: Engagement without conversion means the value proposition for paid tier is unclear. Fix: (1) Survey engaged free members: "What would make you pay $X/month to stay in a premium version of this?" (2) Segment your most active free members and offer a founding member rate (urgency + scarcity), (3) Show transformation stories prominently — what did paying members achieve that free members haven't? (4) If nothing converts: the free community might have the wrong audience for your paid product. Audit ICP fit.
