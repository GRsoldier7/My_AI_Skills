# Social Media Architect — Savant-Level Skill

## Philosophy

Social media mastery = **native content, not reposts** (each platform has its own language), **audience > followers** (10,000 engaged followers > 100,000 passive ones), **hooks are the business** (if they don't stop, nothing else matters), and **consistency compounds** (100 mediocre posts beats 10 perfect ones every time).

---

## 1. LinkedIn (Primary B2B & Founder Platform)

### LinkedIn Algorithm Mechanics (2025)
```
What LinkedIn rewards:
  ✅ Content that generates comments (comments > likes > reactions)
  ✅ Dwell time (people reading/pausing on your post)
  ✅ Meaningful comments (not "Great post!")
  ✅ Posts that get engagement in the first 60-90 minutes
  ✅ Document/carousel posts (highest dwell time of any format)

What LinkedIn suppresses:
  ❌ External links in the post body (kills reach ~70%)
  ❌ "Like and comment" bait engagement tactics
  ❌ Crossposted content from other platforms (detectable)
  ❌ Posts that get ignored for 4+ hours after posting

Posting time (general):
  Best: Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9 AM or 12-1 PM in your target timezone
  Avoid: Friday afternoon, weekends (professionals check-out)
```

### LinkedIn Content Formats
```
Text Post (most reach, simplest):
  - 300-1200 characters
  - Line breaks every 1-2 sentences
  - No external links (put in comments)
  - Hook in first 2 lines (what shows before "see more")
  - End with a question to drive comments

Carousel / Document Post (highest engagement):
  - PDF uploaded directly (not Canva embed)
  - Slide 1: Bold hook/promise (what they'll get)
  - Slides 2-9: One insight per slide, large text, visual
  - Slide 10: CTA + follow prompt
  - 7-12 slides optimal

Native Video:
  - Subtitles mandatory (85% of views are muted)
  - First 3 seconds = hook
  - 60-90 seconds for short form; 3-5 min for deep dives
  - Upload directly (not YouTube link)

Newsletter (LinkedIn):
  - Separate from posts; goes to email
  - Best for thought leadership essays
  - Subscribers get email notification (valuable distribution)
```

### LinkedIn Hook Formula
```
Pattern 1: Counterintuitive statement
  "I stopped posting on LinkedIn for 30 days.
   My followers grew by 2,400.
   Here's why:"

Pattern 2: Specific number
  "I sent 847 cold emails in 60 days.
   12 became customers.
   3 became investors.
   These were the 4 things that made the difference:"

Pattern 3: Mistake/confession
  "I wasted $47,000 on paid ads in 2024.
   Not because the ads were bad.
   Because I hadn't done this first:"

Pattern 4: Contrarian take
  "Your pricing is too low.
   Not because you're cheap.
   Because you're afraid.
   [Here's how I know]"

Pattern 5: "Most X do Y. The best X do Z."
  "Most founders hire to fill roles.
   The best founders hire to remove bottlenecks.
   The difference is everything."
```

## 2. X / Twitter (Thought Leadership + Tech Audience)

### X Algorithm & Strategy (2025)
```
Algorithm rewards:
  ✅ Replies (conversation > passive consumption)
  ✅ Bookmarks (strongest signal: "I want to read this later")
  ✅ Reposts with comments (QTs > reposts > likes)
  ✅ Long-form engagement (reading time signals)
  ✅ Premium/verified accounts (higher distribution weight)

Content formats:
  Single tweet: < 280 characters; hooks, one-liners, observations
  Thread: Multi-tweet essay; numbered 1/ 2/ 3/
  Long post (X Premium): 25,000 character posts; replaces blog for some

Thread architecture:
  Tweet 1: Hook (most important — drives the click to expand)
  Tweets 2-8: Insight/step/example per tweet (value delivery)
  Tweet 9: Summary / key takeaway
  Tweet 10: Call to action (follow, retweet, link to product/newsletter)
```

### Thread Hook Patterns
```
The Bold Claim: "X doesn't work anymore. Here's what does: [thread]"
The List Teaser: "10 tools that replaced my $50K/year team: [thread]"
The Story: "6 months ago I had zero users. Today: 10K. The exact steps: [thread]"
The Mistake: "I lost 40% of my customers in one day. Here's what I learned: [thread]"
The Research: "I studied 200 successful SaaS founders. 7 patterns emerged: [thread]"
```

## 3. YouTube (Highest-Value Audience Platform)

### YouTube Strategy (Compound, Not Viral)
```
Why YouTube compounds:
  - Videos rank in Google Search (double distribution)
  - Video SEO: Title + description keyword matching
  - Watch time compounds into subscribers
  - Library of 50+ videos = 24/7 discovery engine

Content architecture:
  Educational series:  "How to X" — rank for how-to searches
  Opinion/analysis:    "Why X is happening in [industry]" — thought leadership
  Case studies:        "How [customer] did Y with Z" — proof content
  Behind-the-scenes:   Build in public — highest authenticity, high trust

YouTube SEO process:
  1. Keyword research: YouTube autocomplete + TubeBuddy/vidIQ
  2. Title: Primary keyword in first 60 characters
  3. Description: 300+ words; keywords in first 2 lines
  4. Tags: 5-10 relevant keywords
  5. Chapters: Timestamps increase watch time and search ranking
  6. Thumbnail: High contrast, large text, emotion/curiosity face

Video length:
  Tutorial/how-to: 8-15 minutes
  Analysis/opinion: 10-20 minutes
  Documentary: 20-40 minutes
  (Algorithm rewards watch percentage; longer is not always better)
```

### YouTube Script Structure
```
Hook (0-30 seconds):
  - Promise what they'll get
  - Tease the most interesting part (watch retention hook)
  - "By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to [outcome]"

Context (30-90 seconds):
  - Why this matters NOW
  - Your credibility to talk about it (brief)

Body (bulk of video):
  - Deliver on the promise with specific steps/examples
  - B-roll, screenshare, or visual to maintain engagement
  - One idea per chapter; chapters every 3-5 minutes

CTA stack (last 30-60 seconds):
  - Subscribe (specific reason: "for weekly [topic] breakdowns")
  - Like (helps algorithm)
  - Next video recommendation
  - Newsletter or lead magnet (highest-value CTA)
```

## 4. TikTok / Reels / Shorts (Short-Form Video)

### Short-Form Video Framework
```
The first 2 seconds are everything:
  - Grab attention with: Controversy, curiosity gap, bold claim, or visual hook
  - Show, don't tell (demonstrate, not explain)
  - No intro: Start mid-action, mid-sentence, or mid-thought

Content formats that work:
  ✅ Listicles: "3 things I wish I knew before starting a SaaS"
  ✅ Transformations: Before / after / how
  ✅ Reactions: React to a trend or viral moment in your niche
  ✅ Behind-the-scenes: "Day in the life of a [role]"
  ✅ Mistakes: "Stop doing X — do this instead"
  ✅ Stitches/duets: Piggyback on trending content

B2B TikTok (growing):
  - LinkedIn professionals increasingly on TikTok
  - Same formats work but frame professionally
  - Hashtags: #entrepreneurship #businesstips #saas #startup
  - Hook: Workplace/professional struggle → business insight

Repurposing efficiency:
  1. Record once (vertical, 60-90 seconds, high energy)
  2. Post: TikTok → Reels → Shorts (trim to 60s for Shorts)
  3. Same content reaches 3 platforms with 15 minutes total
```

## 5. Consistency Systems

### The 10-Post-Per-Week Solo Creator System
```
Monday (10 min):
  - LinkedIn: Text post with insight from the week

Tuesday (20 min):
  - X/Twitter: Thread from last week's long-form content

Wednesday (30 min):
  - LinkedIn: Carousel (repurpose the X thread into slides)
  - X/Twitter: Single tweet (repurpose one carousel slide)

Thursday (15 min):
  - Short video (TikTok/Reels/Shorts) — talking head on one idea

Friday (20 min):
  - LinkedIn: Question/conversation starter (drives comments)
  - X/Twitter: "Friday wins" post (share a customer win or metric)

Total: ~95 minutes/week = 10 pieces of content across 3 platforms

Stack:
  Scheduling: Typefully (LinkedIn + Twitter) + Later (TikTok + IG)
  Templates:  Canva (carousel slides) with locked brand kit
  Recording:  iPhone + ring light + CapCut for quick mobile edits
```

### The Content Batch Day
```
Once per month (4-hour session):
  1h: Research + ideate 20 content ideas (swipe file input)
  1h: Write 8 LinkedIn posts + 4 X threads
  1h: Record 4 short videos in one session (batch filming)
  1h: Schedule everything in Typefully/Later

Output: One 4-hour session = full month of content
Rule: Never create content day-of. Always have a 1-2 week buffer.
```

## Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Same Content Across All Platforms**
Copy-pasting a LinkedIn post to X, Instagram, and TikTok without adaptation. Each platform has different optimal formats, character limits, audience expectations, and algorithm signals. Cross-posted content underperforms by 40-70% compared to native content.
Fix: Create once, adapt many. Write the long-form LinkedIn post. Then: extract the best line for a tweet, pull the visual summary for an Instagram graphic, record a 60-second vertical video covering the same idea for TikTok. Same idea, native execution for each platform.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Posting Without a Hook**
Starting posts with "I want to share something..." or "Today I was thinking about..." or the company name. The hook must earn the read. No one owes you their attention — you have to take it.
Fix: Write the hook last. Write the content first (the insight, the list, the story). Then ask: "If this is a trailer for the content, what single line would make someone HAVE to keep reading?" That's your hook. A strong hook can make mediocre content perform well; a weak hook makes great content invisible.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Chasing Virality Over Building Audience**
Posting memes, hot takes, or trending topics that have nothing to do with your business. Going viral among people who don't match your ICP inflates vanity metrics without building a useful audience.
Fix: Define your content filter: "Would my ICP find this valuable or interesting?" If yes, publish. If it's only entertaining to the general internet, it's not building the right audience. Every post should serve one of: positioning you as an expert, helping your ICP solve a problem, or making your ideal customer think "this person gets me."

## Failure Modes and Fallbacks

**Failure: Consistent posting but flat follower growth and zero engagement**
Detection: Posting 5x/week for 90 days. Follower count moves <5%. Likes rarely exceed 10. Zero comments.
Fallback: Engagement failure on LinkedIn/X means either wrong audience or wrong content. Audit: (1) Profile — is your headline and about section written for your ICP? (2) Content — is every post about YOUR interests or your ICP's problems? (3) Engagement — are you commenting meaningfully on 10 posts/day from your ICP and potential followers? (4) Network — have you connected with 5 new ICP per day? Growth on LinkedIn is 60% outbound engagement + 40% inbound from good content.

**Failure: YouTube channel with good content but no views**
Detection: 30+ videos published. Average video gets <100 views. Subscriber count flat.
Fallback: YouTube discovery failure is almost always a title/thumbnail problem. Run YouTube Studio analytics: what's your click-through rate (CTR)? If < 3%, your thumbnails aren't compelling. If CTR is 5%+ but average view duration is < 30%, the content doesn't deliver on the promise. Fix: Study the thumbnails of channels with 100K-1M subscribers in your niche. What makes people click? Recreate those patterns with your face/topic. Title: include the exact phrase someone would search for to find this video.
