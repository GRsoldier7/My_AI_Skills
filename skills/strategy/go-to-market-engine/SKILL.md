# Go-to-Market Engine — Savant-Level Skill

## Philosophy

GTM mastery = **ICP precision over broad reach** (a laser, not a floodlight), **one channel to domination before diversifying** (depth beats breadth at every stage), **distribution as the product** (the best product with the worst distribution always loses), and **PMF signals over metrics** (you'll feel PMF — it's pull, not push).

---

## 1. ICP Definition (Ideal Customer Profile)

### ICP Precision Framework
```
Firmographic (B2B):
  Industry:         [Specific vertical, not "technology"]
  Company size:     [Revenue range or employee count]
  Stage:            [Seed / Series A / SMB / Mid-market / Enterprise]
  Geography:        [Country, region, or "remote-first"]
  Tech stack:       [What they use that signals fit]
  Business model:   [SaaS / Agency / eCommerce / etc.]

Psychographic:
  Trigger event:    [What just happened that makes them ready to buy?]
  Pain severity:    [Crisis-level or inconvenience?]
  Budget authority: [Who signs? What's the approval process?]
  Alternatives:     [What are they doing today instead?]
  Success vision:   [What does "solved" look like for them?]

Negative ICP (who NOT to sell to):
  [ ] Price-sensitive early-stage startups with <$10K budget
  [ ] Organizations with 18-month procurement cycles (if you're pre-PMF)
  [ ] Verticals with compliance requirements you can't support yet

// ICP definition test:
// "If I could only send this to 100 people, who would they be?"
// If the answer is vague, the ICP is not sharp enough.
```

### Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) ICP Layer
```
Each ICP has a Job:
  Functional job:     The practical task to accomplish
  Emotional job:      How they want to feel (or stop feeling)
  Social job:         How they want to be seen by others

Example — Productivity SaaS for Engineers:
  Functional:  "I need to get through code reviews faster"
  Emotional:   "I want to feel in control, not buried"
  Social:      "I want my team to see me as organized and reliable"

// JTBD insight: Customers don't buy products, they hire them to do a job.
// Your messaging must speak to the emotional + social job, not just functional.
// "Move faster" (functional) + "Look like the team lead who has it together" (social).
```

## 2. Channel Selection Matrix

### GTM Motion by Stage
```
0 → First 10 customers:
  Method: Direct outreach to YOUR network
  Why: Fastest feedback loop, zero CAC, maximum signal
  How: Direct messages on professional networks, email, communities you're already part of
  Goal: Not to scale — to learn whether you have a real problem

10 → First 100 customers:
  Method: Founder-led sales + content seeding
  Why: Founder's domain authority + early SEO seeds
  How: Outbound to ICP lists + publish in communities where ICP hangs out
  Goal: Find the channel with the best conversion and feedback signal

100 → First 1,000 customers:
  Method: Double down on one proven channel
  Why: Premature channel diversification kills focus
  How: 80% budget on the proven channel; 20% experiment budget
  Goal: Get CAC below LTV/3 on the primary channel

1,000+ customers:
  Method: System + second channel
  Why: Concentration risk on one channel; diversification is now smart
  How: Build the system for channel 1; launch channel 2 experiment
  Goal: Multi-channel growth with CAC < 12-month payback on each
```

### Channel Evaluation Framework
```
Score each channel on 5 dimensions (1-5):

Dimension         Weight  Channel A  Channel B  Channel C
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
CAC potential      30%       _/5        _/5        _/5
Speed to signal    20%       _/5        _/5        _/5
Scalability        20%       _/5        _/5        _/5
ICP concentration  20%       _/5        _/5        _/5
Defensibility      10%       _/5        _/5        _/5
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Weighted score     100%      X.X        X.X        X.X

// Score channels you can actually test in 30 days.
// Don't theorize — run $500 experiments across 3 channels simultaneously.
// Let the data pick the winner.
```

### Channel Deep Dives
```
Content-Led Growth (SEO + thought leadership):
  Time to signal: 3-6 months
  CAC: $10-50 at scale (but slow start)
  Best for: Products with large search volume; complex buying cycles
  Tactics: Topical authority cluster content, pillar pages, comparison pages

Product-Led Growth (PLG):
  Time to signal: Immediate (within product)
  CAC: Near $0 for viral loops
  Best for: Products with "aha moment" discoverable without sales
  Tactics: Free tier, freemium, viral sharing features, in-product referral

Community-Led Growth:
  Time to signal: 3-6 months (to build trust)
  CAC: Near $0 for members who convert
  Best for: Products solving problems professionals discuss online
  Tactics: Genuine participation in existing communities; build your own later

Cold Outbound (email/LinkedIn):
  Time to signal: 2-4 weeks
  CAC: $50-500 depending on conversion
  Best for: B2B with clear ICP and $500+ ACV; high-signal trigger events
  Tactics: Trigger-based (new role, funding, tech adoption), personalized

Paid Acquisition:
  Time to signal: 1-2 weeks
  CAC: Highly variable; can scale or can bleed
  Best for: Clear ICP + landing page conversion data; ACV > $500
  Tactics: LinkedIn (B2B), Meta (B2C/SMB), Google Search (intent)

Partner/Integration:
  Time to signal: 2-4 months (partnership negotiation)
  CAC: Near $0 on partner traffic
  Best for: Products that integrate with larger platform ecosystems
  Tactics: App marketplace listings, co-marketing with complementary tools
```

## 3. Launch Sequencing

### Pre-Launch Phase (T-60 to T-0)
```
T-60 days:
  [ ] ICP defined and validated (20+ customer interviews done)
  [ ] Landing page live with email capture (target: 500 subscribers)
  [ ] Content seeding started in 3 target communities
  [ ] Beta offer crafted: early access, founding member pricing

T-30 days:
  [ ] Beta users onboarded (target: 25-50)
  [ ] 3 case studies or testimonials written from beta
  [ ] Email sequence written: welcome → value → launch announcement
  [ ] Product Hunt listing drafted
  [ ] 5 launch partners identified for day-1 amplification

T-7 days:
  [ ] Full launch email sequence queued
  [ ] Social content calendar set for launch week
  [ ] Product Hunt scheduled (Tuesday-Thursday 12:01 AM PT)
  [ ] Press outreach sent (3-5 targeted journalists)
  [ ] Community posts drafted (post on launch day, never before)

T-0 (Launch Day):
  [ ] Send email to waitlist
  [ ] Post on Product Hunt
  [ ] Personal outreach to 20 highest-value potential customers
  [ ] Cross-post in 3 target communities (authentically)
  [ ] Monitor and respond to every comment
```

### Post-Launch Momentum (Day 1 to Week 8)
```
Week 1: Convert launch interest
  - Personal follow-up to every trial signup within 4 hours
  - Collect feedback from first users daily
  - Fix top 3 onboarding friction points immediately

Week 2-4: Analyze and double down
  - Which channel produced the best conversion? Double spend.
  - What's the most common reason people aren't converting? Fix one per week.
  - Do 5 success calls with users who "get it" — understand the aha moment

Week 4-8: Build the flywheel
  - Write case studies from your first successes
  - Start the primary content channel (newsletter or YouTube)
  - Launch referral or affiliate program
  - Build integration with #1 tool in your customer's stack
```

## 4. Product-Market Fit Measurement

### PMF Signals vs. Metrics
```
The Sean Ellis PMF Test:
  Survey active users: "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?"
  - Very disappointed
  - Somewhat disappointed
  - Not disappointed (it isn't that useful)
  - N/A — I no longer use it

  PMF = >40% say "Very disappointed"
  (Validated across 100+ companies. Below 40% = not PMF yet.)

PMF behavior signals (more reliable than surveys):
  Pull signal:    Customers pulling you forward — asking for more, referring
                  without being asked, protesting when you talk about changes
  Organic growth: Word-of-mouth > all paid channels combined
  Teeth gritter:  Customers use you despite rough UX because value is undeniable
  Support relief: Customer success team asks you to slow signups (overwhelmed)
  Competitor panic: Competitors start copying your positioning

Anti-PMF signals:
  ❌ Users need convincing to come back (push, not pull)
  ❌ Churn > 8%/mo regardless of product improvements
  ❌ Most users came from a single spike (Product Hunt) with no organic follow
  ❌ You're the only one excited — customers are polite but not enthusiastic
```

### PMF Iteration Loop
```
If you don't have PMF:
  1. Narrow the ICP further (remove segments that aren't getting value)
  2. Identify the users who DO get value — talk to them deeply
  3. Build only the features that the high-engagement cohort says is missing
  4. Remove features that complicate the core

Question: "Who are the users who would be VERY disappointed to lose this?
           Can we build a business by only serving them?"
```

## 5. Founder-Led Sales System

### The First 100 Customers Playbook
```
Week 1-2: Network activation
  - Email/DM 50 people in your network who match ICP
  - Subject: "I built something you might find useful — 10 min?"
  - Goal: 10 demo calls booked

Week 3-4: Community seeding
  - Join 5 communities where ICP hangs out (Slack, Discord, Reddit, LinkedIn groups)
  - Add value in 20+ threads before mentioning your product
  - Answer questions where your product is the obvious solution
  - Goal: 5 inbound demo requests

Week 5-8: Outbound expansion
  - Build ICP list: LinkedIn Sales Navigator + Hunter.io + Clay.com
  - Write 5-10 highly personalized outreach templates (trigger-based)
  - Send 20-30 outreach messages per day
  - Goal: 5% positive reply rate (1-2 calls per day)
```

### Demo Call Structure (Founder-Led)
```
1. Confirm fit upfront (2 min):
   "Before I dive in — tell me about [specific pain point]."
   [If no clear pain: "This might not be for you, but let me show you and tell me if it resonates."]

2. Discovery (10 min):
   - Current process / how they solve this today
   - Frequency + severity of the pain
   - What they've tried (and why it didn't work)
   - Who else is affected / involved in decisions

3. Demo — their words (15 min):
   - Use their exact language from discovery
   - "You mentioned that [problem]. Here's how we handle that."
   - Focus on 2-3 features relevant to their specific situation
   - Never show the full product on a first call

4. Next steps (5 min):
   - Trial: "Want to run a trial this week with [specific use case they mentioned]?"
   - Proposal: "I'll send you a quick proposal today. Who else should be on that email?"
   - Never close with "I'll follow up in a few days" — always get a specific next step
```

## Anti-Patterns

**Anti-Pattern 1: Channel Promiscuity at Early Stage**
Running ads, doing SEO, posting on 5 social platforms, cold emailing, and attending conferences simultaneously with a 1-3 person team. Every channel is under-resourced, none get traction, and it's impossible to learn what's working.
Fix: Pick ONE primary channel. Allocate 80%+ of time and budget to it for 90 days. Define success criteria in advance. Only add a second channel after the first one reaches consistent, profitable CAC. One channel to ramen profitable beats five channels all struggling simultaneously.

**Anti-Pattern 2: Launching to Everyone at Once**
Doing a public launch (Product Hunt, press release, email blast to entire list) before validating with a tight cohort. Launch energy is finite — when you spend it before the product is ready, there's no second chance for a first impression.
Fix: Launch in concentric circles. First: personal network (zero risk, maximum learning). Second: warm community. Third: targeted outreach. Only public launch when: (1) beta users love the product, (2) you have 3+ testimonials, (3) you know exactly who the product is for.

**Anti-Pattern 3: Optimizing for Signups Instead of Activated Users**
Measuring success by sign-up count while ignoring activation, retention, and engagement. A 1,000-signup launch that converts 2 people to active users is a failure. A 50-signup launch where 40 become active users is PMF evidence.
Fix: Define "activated" clearly (first key action completed, e.g., first automation run, first project created, first team member invited). Optimize for activation rate, not signup count. Every growth decision should be filtered through: "Does this bring in users more likely to activate, or does it just inflate the top of the funnel?"

## Failure Modes and Fallbacks

**Failure: Product launched to crickets — zero organic traction**
Detection: Launch week drives 200 signups. Week 2: 3 signups. Email open rates drop. No word-of-mouth.
Fallback: Launches don't fail — they reveal. Do immediate post-mortem: who were the 200 signups? How many activated? Why didn't the others? Run 10 "why didn't you use it" interviews with non-activators. Usually the answer is one of: (1) wrong ICP was reached, (2) activation friction is too high, (3) the value proposition isn't clear enough in the product. Fix the biggest blocker, then re-launch to the next concentric circle.

**Failure: CAC on primary channel exceeds LTV**
Detection: Paying customers are coming in, but each customer acquired through paid channels costs more than they're worth over their lifetime.
Fallback: Stop spending on paid acquisition immediately. Analyze: Is the problem the channel (wrong audience) or the product (low LTV from churn)? If churn > 5%/mo: fix retention first, then re-test acquisition. If churn is fine but CAC is high: test different creative, different landing pages, different ICP targeting. If nothing improves CAC to <LTV/3 after 60 days: exit the channel and test organic alternatives.
